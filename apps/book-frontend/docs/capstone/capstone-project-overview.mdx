# Capstone Project Overview

## Overview
The Capstone Project is the final stage of this textbook. It brings together everything you learned about Physical AI, humanoid robotics, simulation, ROS 2, control, perception, and conversational AI. The goal is simple: **design, simulate, and present a complete AI-driven robotics workflow**, similar to how real robotics teams deliver end-to-end systems.

This project demonstrates mastery of engineering thinking, documentation, and system design—exactly what industry expects from a robotics engineer.

---

## Key Concepts
- **Problem Definition** — Choosing a real-world task your humanoid or robot must perform.
- **System Architecture** — Mapping sensors, perception, control, and simulation components.
- **Iterative Prototyping** — Building the solution step by step and improving it.
- **Testing & Evaluation** — Measuring performance in ROS 2 or Isaac simulation.
- **Final Presentation** — Explaining your work like a professional robotics engineer.

---

## Detailed Explanation

### 1. Purpose of the Capstone
Real robotics engineering is not about isolated tasks. It is about **integrating multiple subsystems**—hardware, software, sensing, control, and AI—to accomplish meaningful goals. This Capstone challenges you to take the core ideas of this textbook and apply them to a **realistic robotics scenario**.

### 2. What Makes a Good Capstone?
A great project is:
- **Focused** (not too wide, not too narrow)  
- **Realistic** (can be simulated or logically explained)  
- **Engineering-driven** (clear use of control, perception, planning, or learning)  
- **Well-documented** (diagrams, steps, reasoning, evaluation)

Good examples:
- A humanoid that greets students and answers questions  
- A delivery robot navigating hallways  
- A simulation pipeline for balancing on uneven terrain  
- A perception system detecting objects in a kitchen  
- A ROS 2 control loop for locomotion  

### 3. The Expected Output
By the end of the Capstone, you should have:
- A **problem statement**
- A **diagram of your system**
- A **simulation setup** (Isaac or Gazebo)
- A **ROS 2 node structure**
- A **control or perception pipeline**
- A **brief test report**
- A **final summary** of your engineering decisions

### 4. How This Fits the Course
This Capstone mirrors real robotics workflows. Companies like Tesla, Apptronik, Boston Dynamics, and Agility Robotics evaluate engineers using similar tasks. Completing this Capstone prepares you for:
- Internships  
- Portfolio demonstrations  
- Hackathons  
- Robotics engineering interviews

## Example  
**Capstone Example: Humanoid Assistant in a Classroom**

Goal: A humanoid that answers student questions, detects raised hands, and maintains balance during motion.

Key components:
- **Perception:** Detect hands + face direction using simulated data.
- **Control:** PID balancing + three locomotion behaviors.
- **Conversational AI:** A lightweight RAG engine to answer textbook-based questions.
- **Simulation:** Isaac Sim scene of a classroom with human meshes.
- **Evaluation:** Accuracy of hand detection + stability tests.

This is a *perfect example* of integrating Physical AI into a meaningful scenario.
